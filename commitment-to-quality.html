CDCI Project Management Capability

CDCI Consortium will assume overall responsibility for managing the team of consultants, including budget oversight and quality assurance for all agreed assignment outputs. All substantive outputs will be reviewed by the Team Leader and controlled by the CDCI Project Management Team before submission.

The Team Leader, supported by the CDCI Project Management Team, ensures all deliverables align with the work plan's milestones. A monitoring framework will track progress, ensuring quality, cost, and time adherence. If risks arise, CDCI swiftly evaluates and implements corrective measures.

Key Factors of Strength in CDCI Project Management

CDCI has successfully managed large-scale projects across different sectors and countries through a structured project management system. The responsibility for project implementation is clearly delegated from the Project Director to Team Leaders, ensuring accountability.

  • Internal Organization: Lead experts manage specific project sections, ensuring they meet goals and budgets while being supported by Team Leaders for quality assurance.
  • External Organization: The Team Leader facilitates smooth communication between beneficiary institutions, financiers, and the consulting team to ensure alignment and efficiency.
  • Backstopping Team: CDCI provides technical backup to enhance service delivery effectiveness and address any arising challenges.

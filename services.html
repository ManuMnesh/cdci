Governance and Accountability

CDCI understands the extensiveness of the Governance and Accountability sector as it cuts across both the public and private sectors. Overall, it is the framework of rules, relationships, systems, and processes within and by which authority is exercised and controlled in governments and corporations. Accountability is the mechanism by which institutions (governments and corporations) and those in control are held to account.

CDCI services in this sector include:

  • Public Finance Management (PFM):
    • Budgeting / Medium-Term Expenditure Framework (MTEF) systems
    • Intergovernmental fiscal transfer services
    • Treasury Management in a decentralized system of government
    • Corporate Debt Management services
  • Financial Management Services:
    • Financial and accounting services
    • Receivables and payables maintenance and management services
    • Financial Management Systems Design and implementation
    • Standard operating procedures design and implementation
    • Financial Performance Management review
    • Fiduciary agency services
  • Internal Audit Services
  • Procurement and Contract Management services:
    • Acquisition and assistance services for federal and state agencies
    • Procurement agency services

Agriculture and Food Security

Food security is a growing concern, especially in developing countries. Factors such as limited access to quality agricultural inputs, climate change, disasters, conflict, and a growing global population have worsened food insecurity. The COVID-19 pandemic further disrupted food supply chains.

CDCI collaborates with development agencies such as USAID, the World Bank, and AfDB to provide solutions to agricultural and food security challenges.

CDCI services in this sector include:

  • Agriculture policy and analysis
  • Agribusiness value chain and market systems
  • Private Sector Development

Water, Sanitation, and Health (WASH)

Access to clean water, sanitation, and hygiene (WASH) is critical for health and development. Many countries lack sufficient access to these services. According to UNICEF, 2.2 billion people lack safe drinking water, over half the global population lacks safe sanitation, and more than three billion people lack handwashing facilities.

CDCI partners with agencies globally to mitigate WASH challenges.

  • Development and implementation of community-based WASH management models
  • Development of WASH investment plans
  • Billing management and tariff development
  • Implementation of billing management systems tailored to community needs
  • Public-private partnerships for sustainable WASH services

Trade and Logistics

The WTO Trade Facilitation Agreement (TFA) outlines measures to improve trade efficiency, including transparent laws, streamlined clearance procedures, and reduced administrative barriers.

CDCI works with agencies worldwide to assist governments in implementing TFAs and improving trade facilitation.

  • Feasibility studies on single-window trade facilitation systems
  • Development and analysis of trade policies
  • Trade corridors economic and logistical analysis
